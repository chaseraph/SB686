1.0 Executive Summary

The Department of Public Works submitted the annual budget review on January 15, 2024. While the majority of funds were allocated to standard maintenance, approximately $2.4 million remains unaccounted for in the 'Miscellaneous Contracting' ledger. Editor's Note This is the core issue. The "Miscellaneous" category usually caps at $50k. A $2.4m entry is unprecedented and triggered this entire audit.

The committee reviewed vendor receipts from Q3 and Q4. It was noted that the selection process for the River-Walk project was expedited, bypassing the standard 30-day public bidding window due to "emergency circumstances." Legal Flag State Law (ORS 279C) defines "emergency" strictly as immediate threats to public safety. There was no flooding or structural failure recorded during this period to justify bypassing the bid.

2.0 Vendor Relationships

Analysis of the winning contractors reveals a pattern of consolidation. Three primary firms were awarded 85% of all municipal contracts. Two of these firms share a registered business address with a current member of the oversight board. Key Finding This is a clear conflict of interest. We have verified this address via public tax records. The board member did not recuse themselves from the voting process.

When interviewed, the board member stated that the address match was a clerical error and that they had divested from the company in 2022. However, dividends were paid to their personal account as recently as last month. Update We obtained bank records (see Appendix B) that contradict the board member's testimony.
